REFPCT

Refinery Pressure Compensated Temperature

Pressure compensated temperatures (PCTs) are commonly used in refineries to assist in stabilizing product compositions in separation equipment by compensating for changes in pressure. The measured temperature is adjusted based on changing the current pressure measurement to a base pressure by using vapor pressure curves. In some cases an online analyzer is available to measure the specific component of interest. In this case the analyzer measurement is often applied in a controller that resets a target PCT in a lower level controller (cascade). This routine can be applied to most typical refinery hydrocarbon streams that consist of a mixture of normal paraffin components, valid for boiling ranges between -50 to 500 deg F.
