One-Point Crossover

One-point Crossover is a genetic algorithm reproduction technique that creates two children from two parents by swapping all of each parentâ€™s genes after some randomly selected point. One-point Crossover can produce children that are radically different from either of the parents; however, One-point Crossover will not introduce differences when the swapped parts are the same in each parent.
