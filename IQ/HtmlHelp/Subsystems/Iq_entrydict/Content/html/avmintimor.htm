AVMINTIMOR

Minimum time, in minutes, the analyzer signal must remain outside range before considered good. When the change in subsequent analyzer values violates one of the rate of change limits (AVROCLIMI or AVROCLIMD), the new signal is considered a spike and temporarily flagged as bad. If the analyzer signal stays at the same level for a period longer than the minimum time limit specified by AVMINTIMOR, it is assumed that the process has shifted to a new operating level and all the previous signals which were flagged as spikes in the same direction are changed to a good status.

The count of the amount of time the analyzer signal has stayed outside range is maintained in the AVTIMORI and AVTIMORD entries.

The spike values flagged as bad will not be considered as good at a later time if the minimum time outside range (AVMINTIMOR) value is less than or equal to zero (0).
